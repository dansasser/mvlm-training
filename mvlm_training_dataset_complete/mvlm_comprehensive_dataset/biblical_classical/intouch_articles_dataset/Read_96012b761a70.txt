God’s Guide to a Fruitful Life
A sustained habit of Bible study will transform your mind—and your life.
The Lord told His disciples, “I am the vine, you are the branches; he who remains in Me and I in him, he bears much fruit” (John 15:5). As we carry out Jesus’ plans through the power of the Holy Spirit, two things will become increasingly evident: We will ...
Treasure God’s Word in our heart. By studying the Bible, we learn many important things about the Lord, including His character, plan, and promises. Regular study develops our ability to think biblically and deepens our relationship with Him. One of the indications that we treasure His Word is a change in behavior—our decisions will be guided by His principles, and our actions will reflect the fruit of the Spirit (Galatians 5:22-23).
Adorn ourselves with kindness and truth. These two virtues are to be our constant companions. The Lord wants us to speak truth to one another—and to temper it with loving compassion. The presence of kindness helps to protect relationships from damage; it can also prevent discord and division.
The Christian life is a journey filled with both abundance and challenge (John 10:10; 1 Peter 4:12). At the same time, it is to be characterized by the fruitfulness that comes from following our perfect Guide and Companion.
